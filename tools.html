Tools and Supplies

Paint Pens

The pen you use matters. Different sleeve materials react differently to different inks, so always test on a spare sleeve before committing.

Avoid black markers. Black marks are nearly invisible on most sleeve backs. The only time black works is if every card in the deck uses white-bordered sleeves. For everything else, pick a color with contrast.
Recommended

Uni Posca Paint Pens

The go-to choice for most PRISM users. Water-based acrylic that dries opaque and stays put.

  • PC-1M (extra fine) recommended
  • Wide color range
  • Dries in seconds
  • Won't smear once dry
Alternative

Oil-Based Paint Pens

A good alternative for glossy sleeves where water-based pens struggle to adhere.

  • Extra fine or fine point
  • More limited color selection
  • Takes longer to dry
  • Strong odor when wet
Budget

Acrylic Paint Pen Sets

Budget Amazon sets work in a pinch. Quality varies brand to brand.

  • Often includes 24+ colors
  • Inconsistent flow
  • May need multiple coats
  • Good for trying the system cheaply
Precision

Pentel Matte Hop Gel Pens

These have so much pigment they're surprisingly visible on sleeves, and they produce the cleanest, thinnest lines of any option here.

  • Fewer color options
  • More expensive
  • Easy to wipe off if you make a mistake
  • Can rub off on frequently moved cards
Avoid brush-tip pens if possible. They make it hard to keep your lines consistent, even with the Spirit Guide.

Sleeve Recommendations

Most perfect-fit inner sleeves work well. We recommend:

  • Dragon Shield Perfect Fit Inners
  • KMC Perfect Hards
  • Mlikero Perfect Fit Inners (budget option)

Support your local game store, or use the affiliate links above to help us cover hosting costs and keep improving PRISM.

Double-sleeving: Mark the inner sleeve on the card-face side. The marks are subtle and won't be felt through the outer sleeve.

PRISM Spirit Guide

The Spirit Guide is a marking jig that holds your sleeve in place with guides for each stripe position. It makes marking faster, more consistent, and lets you work one-handed thanks to its magnetic hold.

Storage and Organization

Once you're sharing cards across decks, a good storage system saves you time at the table.

For smaller collections (8–10 decks), the GameGenic Lair 600+ works well and has room for tokens, dice, and extras. For larger pools, the Ultimate Guard Arkhive 1000+ gives you more space. In general, avoid boxes with too many built-in deck dividers. How you divide your PRISM in storage will depend on how your cards overlap across decks, so flexibility matters.

Pro tip: Store your shared cards in a central "pool" box, organized by stripe grouping (shared cards first, then by color) or by card type. When you're building a deck for the night, pull from the pool. Return everything when you're done.
