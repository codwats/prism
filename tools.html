Paint Pens

The right markers make all the difference. Here's what works best:

Recommended

Uni Posca Paint Pens

The gold standard for sleeve marking. Water-based acrylic that dries opaque and stays put.

  • PC-1M (extra fine) or PC-3M (fine) tips
  • Wide color range
  • Dries in seconds
  • Won't smear once dry
Alternative

Sharpie Oil-Based

Good alternative, especially for glossy sleeves where water-based pens struggle.

  • Extra fine or fine point
  • More limited colors
  • Takes longer to dry
  • Strong odor when wet
Budget Option

Acrylic Paint Pen Sets

Amazon basics sets work in a pinch. Quality varies, so test thoroughly.

  • Often includes 24+ colors
  • Inconsistent flow
  • May need multiple coats
  • Good for trying the system cheaply
Tip: Whatever you choose, always test on a spare sleeve first. Different sleeve materials react differently to various inks.

Sleeve Recommendations

Most matte sleeves work great with paint pens. Glossy sleeves can be trickier.

Best for Marking

  • Dragon Shield Matte
  • Ultimate Guard Katana
  • KMC Hyper Matte
  • Gamegenic Matte Prime

Works With Oil-Based

  • Dragon Shield Classic (glossy)
  • Ultra Pro Eclipse (glossy)
  • Most glossy/shiny sleeves
Double-sleeving: Mark the inner sleeve on the card face side. The marks are subtle and won't be felt through the outer sleeve.

Print Settings

  • Material: PLA or PETG
  • Layer height: 0.2mm
  • Infill: 20%
  • Supports: Not required
  • Print time: ~45 minutes
No printer? Many local libraries have 3D printers, or check services like Craftcloud or local makerspaces.

Organization Systems

Once you're sharing cards across decks, you'll want a good system for storing and swapping.

Card Storage

  • BCW Card House — Great for storing your shared card pool
  • Ultra Pro Alcove Flip — Premium option with dividers
  • BCW Shoe Box — Budget bulk storage

Deck Boxes

  • Ultimate Guard Boulder — Compact, stackable
  • Gamegenic Sidekick — Good capacity + tokens
  • Dragon Shield Nest — Easy access design
Pro tip: Store your shared cards in a central "pool" box, organized alphabetically or by type. When deck-building, pull from the pool and return cards when done playing.
