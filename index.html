Frequently Asked Questions

What about basic lands?

Basics aren't singleton, so PRISM tracks the maximum quantity you need across all decks. If Deck A needs 12 Islands and Deck B needs 5, you need 12 total. All 12 get Deck A's mark. 8 of them also get Deck B's mark. The "Basics by Deck" filter shows the exact count for each deck.

 What markers should I use?

Paint pens work best on most sleeves. Look for fine-tip acrylic paint pens in a variety of colors. PRISM defaults to colors that match standard paint pen sets. See the Tools page for specific recommendations.

 What about all my extra cards now?

Sell them. Once your sleeves are marked, you only need one copy of each card. Your duplicates become trade fodder or cash. Many players recoup the cost of their marking supplies by selling extras.

 I have 45 commander decks. Can I still use PRISM?

Each PRISM supports 32 decks. For larger collections, create multiple PRISMs. You might organize them by color identity, power level, or sleeve color.

 Where is my data stored?

All PRISM data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server. You can export your data as JSON anytime for backup or to move between devices.

 Can I reorder the stripe positions?

Yes. Use the Move button on each deck card to open the visual slot picker, or use the Export tab's dropdown list for bulk reordering. Helpful if you want to group decks by color identity or put your most-played decks in easy-to-spot positions.

 Is this tournament legal?

Yes. According to MTR 3.12, cards are only considered "marked" if the marking makes it possible to identify the card without seeing its face. Sleeve edge marks don't add noticeable thickness or affect the card's profile in the deck. If you have to look at it from a specific angle to notice anything, it's fine.
