Sleeve Marking Guide

Everything you need to know about marking your sleeves with the PRISM system.

Quick Overview

PRISM uses colored marks on the top edge of your sleeves to indicate which decks a card belongs to. Each deck gets:

  • A unique color — Choose from 23 default colors or customize
  • A stripe position — Position 1-32 from left to right on the sleeve edge

When you fan your cards, you can instantly see which deck(s) each card belongs to by the colored marks on top.

Step-by-Step Marking

1. Prepare Your Workspace

  • Work on a clean, flat surface
  • Have paper towels handy for wiping pen tips
  • Keep your PRISM results open on a screen or print the guide
  • Sort your cards by the PRISM results order (most shared first works well)

2. Test Your Markers

  • Always test on a spare sleeve first!
  • Check that the color shows up clearly
  • Make sure the ink adheres and doesn't smear once dry
  • Some sleeve materials work better with certain pen types

3. Mark Consistently

  • Mark the top edge of each sleeve (the closed end)
  • Keep marks in consistent positions — use a jig or ruler if helpful
  • Small dots or short lines work well (2-3mm)
  • Let each color dry before adding the next to prevent smearing

4. Work Through Your List

  • Use the search bar to find cards as you go
  • Check "Done" as you mark each card
  • Filter by deck to focus on one deck's cards at a time
  • Start with shared cards (they appear in multiple decks, so you mark once)

Pro Tips

Let It Dry!

Give marks 10-15 seconds to dry before handling. Paint pens especially need a moment to set. Impatience leads to smeared marks.

Double-Sleeving?

Mark the outer sleeve, not the inner. If you mark inner sleeves, they'll be hidden by the outer sleeve's edge.

Consider a Marking Jig

A simple jig with position guides makes marking faster and more consistent. Check the Tools page for 3D printable options.

Batch by Color

Instead of switching pens for each card, do all marks for one color first. This is faster and lets the previous color dry.

Handling Basic Lands

Basic lands are special because they're not singleton — you can have as many as you want in a deck. PRISM handles this by:

  1. Tracking the maximum — If Deck A needs 12 Islands and Deck B needs 8, you need 12 total
  2. Marking all of them — All 12 Islands get marks for both Deck A and Deck B
  3. Using "Basics by Deck" — This filter shows exactly how many of each basic each deck needs

Tip: Consider using different art for each deck's basics. It makes sorting easier and adds personality!

Adding New Decks

When you build a new deck:

  1. Add the deck to your existing PRISM
  2. Use "Filter by Deck" to see only cards in the new deck
  3. Cards already in your collection just need the new deck's mark added
  4. New cards need all their marks (check other decks they appear in)
Tip: The "Done" checkbox resets when you add a new deck, so you can track your progress marking the new deck's cards.
