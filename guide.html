Sleeve Marking Guide

Share your staples across every deck. PRISM tracks what goes where so you don't have to.

How PRISM Markings Work

PRISM uses colored marks on the right edge of your sleeves to show which decks a card belongs to. Each deck gets:

  • A unique color - choose from 23 defaults or set your own
  • A stripe position - 1 through 32, left to right along the sleeve edge

Fan your cards out and you can see at a glance which deck(s) each card belongs to.

Marking Your Sleeves

1. Set Up Your Workspace

  • Work on a clean, flat surface, an old beat up playmat works well.
  • Keep paper towels, and cotton swabs nearby for touch-ups.
  • Have your PRISM results open on a screen, or print your guide.

2. Test Your Markers

  • Always test on a spare sleeve first!
  • Check that the color shows up clearly
  • Make sure the ink adheres and doesn't smear once dry
  • Some sleeve materials work better with certain pen types
Let marks dry before sleeving.
Give them 2-3 minutes, especially paint pens. Rushing this step leads to smeared marks and re-dos.

3. Mark Consistently

  • Mark the right edge of each sleeve.
  • Use the PRISM Sprit Guide as a jig or ruler.
  • Small dots or short lines work well (3-7mm).
Use our PRISM Spirit Guide
A marking jig with position guides makes the whole process faster and more consistent. It holds the sleeve in place so you can add all colors for that card at once, rather than batching by color. You can find the 3D-printable PRISM Spirit Guide on the Tools page.

4. Work Through Your List

  • Start with shared cards, since they appear in multiple decks and only need to be marked once.
  • Check "Done" as you mark each card.
  • Use the search bar to find cards as you go.
    •     or
  • Filter by deck to focus on one deck at a time.
PRISM is designed to be Double-Sleeved
Mark the inner sleeve on the card-face side. The marks are small (around 7mm) and won't be felt through the outer sleeve, so your cards stay tournament legal.

Handling Basic Lands

Basics work differently because they aren't singleton. You can run as many as you want in a deck, so PRISM handles them with a few extra rules.

PRISM tracks the maximum count across your decks. If Deck A needs 12 Islands and Deck B needs 8, you need 12 total. All 12 get Deck A's mark. Only 8 of them get Deck B's mark. The "Basics by Deck" filter shows exactly how many of each basic land each deck needs.

One more thing: consider using different art or sets for each deck's basics. It makes sorting faster and gives each deck its own feel.

Batch by Color
Instead of switching pens for every card, do all marks for one color first. It's faster, and it gives the previous color time to dry. If you're using the PRISM Spirit Guide, you can skip this. The jig uses magnets to hold sleeves in place, so you can work through cards one-handed and add all colors per sleeve as you go. Print or buy multiple guides and set up an assembly line for even faster marking.

Same Commander, Different Power Levels

If you run the same commander at multiple brackets or budgets, you'll want a way to tell those decks apart beyond just the deck color. Two approaches work well.

  • Dots next to the stripe. Add a small dot beside the right-edge stripe to indicate power level. One dot for casual, two for mid, three for high, or whatever system makes sense for your group. This keeps everything on the same edge and is easy to read at a glance.
  • Secondary stripes on the left edge. Use the left side of the sleeve for a second set of marks that indicate bracket or budget tier. This gives you more visual separation and avoids crowding the right edge if you're already sharing cards across several decks.

Either method works. Pick whichever feels more natural for how many variants you're tracking. If you only have two versions of a commander, dots are simpler. If you're running three or more, left-edge stripes give you more room.

Adding New Decks

When you build a new deck:

  1. Add the deck to your existing PRISM
  2. Use "Filter by Deck" to see only the cards in the new deck.
  3. Cards already in your collection just need the new deck's color added.
  4. New cards need all their marks, so check whether they appear in other decks too.
Tip: The "Done" checkbox resets when you add a new deck, so you can track your progress on the new markings from scratch.
