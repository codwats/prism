Sleeve Marking Guide

Share your staples across every deck. PRISM tracks what goes where so you don't have to.

How PRISM Marks Work

PRISM uses colored marks on the edge of your sleeves to show which decks a card belongs to. Whether you mark the left or right edge depends on how you fan your cards. Pick whichever side is visible when you fan, and stay consistent.

Each deck gets a unique color (choose from 23 defaults or set your own) and a stripe position (1 through 32 along the sleeve edge). Fan your cards and you can see at a glance which decks each card belongs to.

Importing Your Decks

From Moxfield or Archidekt

Paste your deck URL into the import field and PRISM pulls the deck name, commander, and full card list. Make sure your deck is set to Public on the source site or the import will fail silently.

Manual entry

Type or paste a decklist in standard MTGO format (one card per line, quantity followed by card name). PRISM parses it, deduplicates, and flags any card names it can't find via Scryfall.

What happens after import

PRISM analyzes your card pool immediately. The Results tab shows every card across all your decks, sorted by how many decks share it. Cards in two or more decks appear in your pool. Cards in only one deck are core to that deck. This distinction matters when you start marking: pool cards need multiple stripe colors, core cards need only one.

Understanding Your Results

Once you've imported at least two decks, the Results tab shows your full card analysis.

Pool vs. core

  • Pool cards appear in two or more decks. These are the cards PRISM saves you money on. Each one needs a stripe mark for every deck it belongs to.
    Optionally add a modifier dot from the Spirit Guide to easily identify all pool cards.
  • Core cards appear in only one deck. They still need a stripe mark, but only one color.

The stats bar at the top shows your total card count and pool count. The bigger your pool relative to your total, the more value PRISM is providing.

The overlap matrix

The Overlap Matrix shows how many cards each pair of decks shares. High overlap between two decks means those cards need marks for both. Low overlap means those decks are mostly independent. This is useful for deciding which decks to bring to a game night: pick decks with low overlap and you carry fewer total cards.

Filtering and sorting

Use "Filter by Deck" to show only cards in a specific deck. Click column headers to sort by card name, number of decks, or stripe assignments. The search bar filters by card name in real time.

Removed cards

If you remove a card from a deck after marking, it moves to the Removed list. This is a reminder that the physical sleeve still has a mark you may want to clean off. Once you've handled it, dismiss the removed card.

Marking Your Sleeves

Finalize your decklists before you start marking. Do not edit your decks during the marking process. Changing cards mid-session leads to incorrect counts, missed marks, and confusion. If you realize a change is needed, write it down and make the edit after you've finished the initial marking pass.

1. Set up your workspace

  • Work on a clean, flat surface. An old playmat works well.
  • Keep paper towels and cotton swabs nearby for touch-ups.
  • Have your PRISM results open on a screen, or print your guide.

2. Test your markers

Always test on a spare sleeve first. Check that the color is visible against your sleeve backs (see the Tools page for recommendations). Make sure the ink sticks and doesn't smear once dry. Some sleeve materials work better with certain pen types, so test before committing.

Let marks dry before sleeving.
Give them 2-3 minutes, especially paint pens. Rushing this step leads to smeared marks and re-dos.

3. Mark consistently

  • Mark the same edge of each sleeve (left or right, whichever you chose).
  • Use the PRISM Spirit Guide as a jig or ruler.
  • Small dots or short lines work well. Aim for 3-7mm.
Use the Spirit Guide.
A marking jig with position guides makes the process faster and more consistent. It holds the sleeve in place so you can add all colors for that card at once rather than batching by color. Find the 3D-printable STL on the Tools page.

4. Work through your list

Use the search bar to find cards as you go. Check "Done" as you finish each one. Filter by deck to focus on one deck at a time. Start with pool cards (cards shared across multiple decks) since they need a mark for every deck they belong to. Core cards only appear in one deck, so they're quicker.

Mark the inner sleeve on the card-face side.
The marks are small (around 7mm) and won't be felt through the outer sleeve. Your cards stay tournament legal.

Handling Basic Lands

PRISM tracks the maximum count across your decks. If Deck A needs 12 Islands and Deck B needs 8, you need 12 total. All 12 get Deck A's mark. Only 8 get Deck B's mark. The "Basics by Deck" filter shows exactly how many of each basic each deck needs.

Or... Consider using different art or sets for each deck's basics. It makes sorting faster and gives each deck its own feel.

Same Commander, Different Power Levels

If you run the same commander at multiple brackets or budgets, you need a way to tell those decks apart beyond the deck color. Two approaches work well.

  • Dots next to the stripe. Add a small dot beside the stripe to indicate power level. No dot for casual, add a dot for higher bracket, or do a different color for more. Keeps everything on the same edge and is easy to read at a glance.
  • Secondary stripes on the other edge. Use the opposite side of the sleeve for a second set of marks that indicate bracket or tier. More visual separation, avoids crowding your main marking edge.

Pick whichever fits how many variants you're tracking. Two versions of a commander? Dots are simpler. Three or more? The other edge gives you more room.

Deck Variants (Split Groups)

Sometimes one deck slot holds multiple versions. A Simic shell that runs three different commanders. A Zur list with a casual build and a competitive one. PRISM handles this with split groups.

How splitting works

When you split a deck, the original becomes the parent and each variant becomes a child. All children share the parent's Side A stripe position and color. Each variant gets its own Side B mark on the opposite sleeve edge to distinguish it from the other variants.

Split styles

You have two options for how variants are marked on Side B:

  • Stripes. Each variant gets a unique color stripe on the opposite edge of the sleeve. Clear and easy to read, but uses more stripe positions.
  • Dots. Variant 1 gets no dot (just the shared Side A stripe). Variant 2 gets one colored dot next to the Side A stripe. Variant 3 gets two dots. Keeps everything on one edge but requires closer inspection.

Pick stripes if you have 2-3 variants and plenty of unused positions. Pick dots if you're running tight on positions or want to keep all marks on one edge.

Creating a split

On the Decks tab, click the split icon on any deck card. Choose how many variants you want (2-8) and which split style to use. Each variant gets its own card list, commander, and bracket rating. Cards shared across all variants keep the parent's Side A mark. Cards unique to a variant get both Side A and their variant's Side B mark.

Reordering Stripe Positions

Stripe positions determine where on the sleeve edge each deck's mark appears. Position 1 is at one end, position 32 at the other. The order matters because you want your most-played decks in the most visible positions.

Using the slot picker

Click the Move button on any deck card to open the visual slot picker. It shows all 32 positions with the current assignments. Tap an empty slot to move the deck there, or tap an occupied slot to swap positions with that deck.

Bulk reordering

The Export tab has a dropdown list that lets you reorder multiple decks at once. Drag decks up or down in the list to rearrange their stripe positions.

Stripe starting corner

By default, stripe position 1 starts at the top of the sleeve edge. In the deck settings, you can change the starting corner to top-left, top-right, bottom-left, or bottom-right. This affects the card preview display but not your stored data. Pick whichever corner is most visible when you fan your cards and don't change it after you've started marking.

Adding New Decks

When you build a new deck:

  1. Add the deck to your existing PRISM.
  2. Assign a color and confirm its stripe position. New decks get the next available position by default.
  3. Use "Filter by Deck" to see only the new deck's cards.
  4. Cards already in your collection just need the new deck's color added to their sleeve.
  5. New cards need all their marks. Check whether they appear in other decks too, because they may need multiple stripes.
The "Done" checkbox resets when you add a new deck, so you can track progress on the new markings from scratch.

Removing a deck

Deleting a deck removes it from your PRISM and frees its stripe position. Cards that were only in that deck will move to the Removed list as a reminder that their sleeves still carry marks. Cards shared with other decks keep their remaining marks.

Exporting Your Data

Printable marking guide

The most common export. Generates a formatted reference you can print and bring to your marking session. Each card shows its name, which decks it belongs to, and which stripe colors and positions to apply. Print it, check cards off as you go.

CSV export

A spreadsheet-compatible file with one row per card. Useful if you want to do your own analysis, track inventory, or build a custom workflow.

JSON export

The raw PRISM data structure. Use this for backups, transferring between devices, or if you're building something on top of PRISM's data. Import a JSON file back into the app to restore a full PRISM with all decks and settings.

Managing Multiple PRISMs

If you have more than 32 decks, or if you want to organize your collection into separate groups, create multiple PRISMs.

Each PRISM is independent: its own deck list, its own stripe positions, its own marking guide. You might separate by sleeve color (all black-sleeved decks in one PRISM, all colored sleeves in another), by power level, or by format once non-Commander formats are supported.

Switch between PRISMs from the header. Your current PRISM is always auto-saved before switching.
