Sleeve Marking Guide

Share your staples across every deck. PRISM tracks what goes where so you don't have to.

1How marks work

Each deck gets a unique color and a stripe position (1 through 48) on the sleeve edge. Fan your cards and the marks show which decks every card belongs to.

Mark whichever edge is visible when you fan, left or right, and stay consistent.

2Import your decks

  • From a URL. Paste a Moxfield or Archidekt link and PRISM pulls the name, commander, and card list. The deck must be set to Public.
  • Manual entry. Paste a decklist in MTGO format (quantity, then card name). PRISM parses it and flags names it can't find on Scryfall.

PRISM analyzes your pool immediately: cards in two or more decks are POOL, cards in one deck are CORE.

Screenshot: Add a Deck with a Moxfield URL pasted

3Read your results

  • POOL cards need a stripe for every deck they belong to. These are the cards PRISM saves you money on.
  • CORE cards need one stripe.
  • The Overlap Matrix counts shared cards per deck pair. Bring low-overlap decks to game night and you carry fewer cards.
  • Cards removed after marking land in the Removed list until you clean the sleeve.
Screenshot: Results tab with POOL rows highlighted

4Mark your sleeves

Finalize your decklists first. Mid-session edits cause wrong counts and missed marks. Write changes down, make them after the pass.
  • Test your pen on a spare sleeve. Check visibility and smearing (see Tools for pens that work).
  • Mark the inner Perfect Fit sleeve, same edge every time. Short lines, 3 to 7mm.
  • Work through the list with search, check Done as you go. Start with POOL cards, they need the most stripes.
Use the Spirit Guide. It holds the sleeve and shows every position, so you paint all of a card's colors in one pass. Until the STL lands on Tools, any straight-edge jig works. Let marks dry 2 to 3 minutes before sleeving. Double-sleeve after: the outer sleeve protects the mark and keeps your cards tournament legal.

5Basic lands and variants

Basics: PRISM tracks the maximum across decks. Deck A needs 12 Islands, Deck B needs 8? You mark 12 total, and only 8 carry Deck B's stripe. The Basics by Deck filter shows the counts.

Variants: split a deck to run multiple builds in one slot. All variants share the parent's Side A stripe. Stripes (2 to 8 variants) add a Side B mark on the other edge; dots (exactly 2) add a colored dot next to the Side A stripe and keep everything on one edge.

Photo: two variants told apart by dots next to the stripe

6Reorder stripe positions

Put your most-played decks in the most visible slots. Click Move on any deck card to open the slot picker: tap an empty slot to move there, tap an occupied one to swap.

The stripe starting corner and position numbers live in Settings (the gear in the header). Pick a corner before you start marking and don't change it after.

Screenshot: the visual slot picker with 48 positions

7Export your guide

  • Printable guide (Results tab, Export menu): every card with its colors and slots, plus the deck legend. Print it and check cards off.
  • CSV for spreadsheets and your own workflows.
  • JSON backup (the menu next to your PRISM name): full backup for restores and device moves.
